THE CODE OF CIVIL PROCEDURE OF NORTH CAROLINA. WHEREAS, it is ordained by
Section 1, Article IV of the Constitution of North Carolina, that: “The
distinction between actions at law and suits in equity and the forms of all
such actions and suits shall be abolished, and there shall be in this State
but one form of action for the enforcement or protection of private rights or
the redress of I private wrongs, which shall be denominated a civil action; and
every action prosecuted by the people of the State as a party against a person
charged with a public offense shall be termed a criminal action. Feigned issues
shall also be abolished, and the fact, at issue tried by order of court before a
jury.” . The General Assembly of the State of North Carolina, for the purpose of
