IN1843-020310

Whenever it shall appear by satisfactory proof, by affidavit to any court
or judge authorized by law to grant a writ of habeas corpus, that any one is
illegally held in custody, confinement, or restraint, and that there is good
reason to believe that such person will be carried out of the jurisdiction
of such court or judge before whom the application is made, or will suffer
some irreparable injury before compliance with a writ of habeas corpus can
be enforced, such court or judge may cause a warrant to be issued, reciting
the facts and directed to any sheriff, coroner, or constable of the county,
commanding such officer to take such person thus held in custody, confinement,
or restraint, and forthwith bring him or her before such court or judge, to be
dealt with according to law. Sacr. 35. Such court or judge may also, if the same
be deemed necessary, insert in such warrant a command for the, apprehension of
the person charged with such illegal detention . or restraint.
