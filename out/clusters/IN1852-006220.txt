IN1852-006220

637. Whenever a complaint is filed for the foreclosure of a mortgage upon which
there shall be due any interest, or instalment of the principal, and there are
other instalments not due, if the defendant pay into court the principal and
interest due, with costs, at any time before final judgment, the complaint shall
be dismissed; if such payment be made after final judgment, proceedings thereon
shall be stayed, subject to be enforced upon a subsequent default in the payment
of any instalment of the principal, or interest thereafter becoming due. In the
final judgment, the court shall direct at what time and upon what default any
subsequent execution shall issue.
