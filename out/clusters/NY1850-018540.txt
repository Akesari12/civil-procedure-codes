NY1850-018540

1697. When the terms of an agreement have been intended in a different sense
by the different parties to it, that sense is to prevail, against either party,
in which he supposed the other understood it; and when different constructions
of a provision are otherwise equally proper, that is to be taken which is most
favorable to the party, in whose favor the provision was made.
