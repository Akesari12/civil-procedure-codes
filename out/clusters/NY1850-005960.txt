NY1850-005960

547. A receiver, before entering upon his duties, must be sworn—faithfully
to discharge his trust, to the best of his ability, and must also file with
the clerk of the court, an undertaking in writing, of one or more sufficient
sureties, in a specified sum, fixed by the court or officer, to the effect that
he will faithfully discharge the duties of receiver, and will obey the orders of
the court or officer in respect thereto. The sureties must justify in the same
manner as bail upon an arrest.

NY1853-005820

540. A receiver, before entering upon his duties, must be sworn faithfully
to discharge his trust, to the best of his ability: and must also file with
the clerk of the court, an undertaking in writing, of one or more sufficient
sureties, in a specified sum, fixed by the court or officer, to the effect that
he will faithfully discharge the duties of receiver, and will obey the orders
of the court or officer in respect thereto. The sureties must justify in the
same manner as bail upon an arrest. The compensation of the receiver may be
prescribed by the court.
