479. When a peremptory mandate has been issued and directed to any inferior
tribunal, corporation, board, or person, if it appear to the Court that any
member of such tribunal, corporation, or board, or such person upon whom the
writ has been personally served, has, without just excuse, refused or neglected
to obey the same, the Court may, upon motion, impose a fine not exceeding one
thousand dollars. In case of persistance in a refusal of obedience, the Court
may order the party to be imprisoned for a period not exceeding three months,
and may take any orders necessary and proper. for the complete enforcement of
the writ. If a fine be imposed upon a judge or oHicer who draws a salary from
the State or County, a certified copy of the order shall be forwarded to the
Comptroller, or County Treasurer, as the case may be, and the amount thereof may
be retained from the salary of such judge or officer. Such judge or oHicer for
his willful disobedience shall also be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor in office.
