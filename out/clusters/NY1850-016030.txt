NY1850-016030

1469. When a contempt is committed in the immediate view and presence of the
court or officer, it may be punished summarily, for which an order must be made
reciting the facts as occurring in such immediate view, and presence, adjudging
that the person proceeded against, is thereby guilty of a contempt, and that he
be punished as therein described. Such punishment, however, cannot exceed that
prescribed by section 1478. Where the contempt is not committed in the immediate
view and presence of the court, an affidavit must be presented to the court, or
officer, of the facts constituting the contempt.
