NY1850-018180

1662. The law does not require demonstration, that is, such a degree of proof
as, excluding possibility of error, produces absolute certainty; because such
proof is rarely possible. Moral certainty only is required, or that degree of
proof, which produces conviction in an unprejudiced mind.
