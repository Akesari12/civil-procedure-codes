NY1876-004210

390. Where a cause of action, which does not involve the title to or possession
of real property Within the State, accrues against a person, who is not then
a resident of the State, an action cannot be brought thereon in a court of the
State, against him or his personal representative, after the expiration of the
time, limited, by the laws of his residence, for bringing a like action, except
by aresident of the State and in one of the following cases: 1. Where the cause
of action originally accrued in favor of a resident of the State. . 2. Where,
before the expiration of the time so limited, the person, in Whose favor it
originally accrued, was or became a resident of the State; or the cause of
action was assigned to, and thereafter continuously owned by, a resident of the
State.
