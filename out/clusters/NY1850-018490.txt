NY1850-018490

1692. In the construction of a statute, the intention of the legislature,
and in the construction of an instrument, the intention of the parties, is
to be pursued if possible; and when a general and particular provision are
inconsistent, the latter is paramount to the former. So a particular intent will
control a general one, that is inconsistent with it.
