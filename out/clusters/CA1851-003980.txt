an order or decree admitting a will to probate, or refusing the same : 2d.
From an order setting apart property, or making an allowance for the widow or
children : 3d. From an order granting letters testamentary or of administration,
or appointing a guardian of an infant, or of an insane person, or of a person
incompetent to manage his property, or refusing to grant such letters, or to
make such appointment, or making such letters or appointment. 4th. From an order
directing the sale or conveyance of real property: 5th. From an order or decree
by which a debt, claim, legacy, or distributive share is allowed, or payment
thereof directed ; or by which , such allowance or direction is refused : ‘ 6th.
From an order made on the settlement of an executor, administrator, or guardian.
