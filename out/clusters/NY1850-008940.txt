NY1850-008940

811. Any party may, and if required by the court, must when the evidence is
closed, submit in distinct and concise propositions, the conclusions of fact
which he claims to be established, or the conclusions of law, which he desires
to have adjudged, or both; they may be written and handed to the court, or at
the option of the court, oral and entered in the judge’s minutes; but in either
case they must be entered with any exceptions that may be taken, if either party
require it.
