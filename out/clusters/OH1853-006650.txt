OH1889-000170

4960. Clerk to file and preserve papers. The clerk shall file together, and
carefully preserve in his office, all papers delivered to him for that purpose,
in every action or proceeding. [51 v. 57, \ 585 ; S. & C. 1129.] A paper is
considered filed when delivered to and received by the proper officer, 43 O.
S. 57 ; when placed in the clerk's office and either strung upon a thread or
laid in a drawer or pigeon hole, 4 O. 88. The indorsement by the clerk is but
evidence of such filing, 43 O. S. 57.
