58. The, marine court of the city of New-York shall have jurisdiction in the
following cases, and no other: 1. In actions similar to those in which courts
of justices of the peace have jurisdiction, as provided by sections 46 and 47.

New-York, where the penalty or forfeiture shall exceed twenty-five dollars, and
not exceed one hundred dollars. 3. In an action between a person belonging to
a vessel in the merchant service, and the owner, master or commander thereof,
demanding compensation for the performance, or damages for the violation, of a
contract for services on board such vessel, (luring a voyage performed, in whole
or in part, or intended to be performed, by such vessel, though the sum demanded
exceed one hundred dollars. 4. In an action by or against any person belonging
to or on board of a vessel in the merchant service, for an asault anti battery
or false imprisonment, committed on board such vessel, upon the high seas, or
in a place without the United States, of which the ordhary courts of law of this
state have jurisdiction though the (amages demanded exceed one hundred dollars.
But nothing in this or the last preceding subdivision of this section, shall
give the court power to proceed in any of the cases therein referred to, as a
court of admiralty or maritime jurisdiction.
