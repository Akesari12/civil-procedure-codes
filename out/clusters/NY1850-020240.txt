NY1850-020240

1854. A witness must answer questions legal and pertinent to the matter in
issue, though his answer may establish a claim against himself. But he need not
give an answer, which will have a tendency to subject him to punishment for a
felony. Nor need he give an answer, which will have a direct tendency to degrade
his character, unless it be to the very fact in issue, or to a fact from which
the fact in issue would be presumed. But a witness must answer as to the fact of
his previous conviction for felony.
