Whenever the justice shall be satisfied that a jury
sworn in any cause before him cannot agree in their verdict
after having consulted upon it areasonable time, he may
discharge them and continue the cause, and may, if required
by either party, proceed to strike another jury as hereinbefore provided; the cause shall be continued to such time as
the justice thinks reasonable, unless the parties or their attorneys agree on a longer or shorter time, or unless they may
agree that the justice may render judgment on the evidence

already heard before him.
