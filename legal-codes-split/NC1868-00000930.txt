—Allegatlon not denied; when to be deemed true.

Every material allegation of the complaint not controverted by the answer, as prescribed in section ninety-seven, and
every material allegation of new matter in the answer, constituting a counterclaim, not controverted by the reply, as
prescribed in section one hundred and one, shall, for the purposes of the action, be taken as true. But the allegation of
new matter in the answer, not relating‘to a counterclaim,
or of new matter in reply, is to be deemed controverted by

the adverse party as, upon a direct denial or avoidance, as
the case may require.
