In an action for the recovery of specific personal property, if the property has not been
delivered to the plaintifff, or the defendant, by his answer, claims a return thereof, the jury shall assess
the value of the property, if their verdict be in favor of the plaintifff; or if they find in favor of the
defendant, and that he is entitled to a return thereof, and may at the same time assess the damages, if

any are claimed, in the complaint or answer which the prevailing party has sustained by reason of the
detention or taking and withholding such property.
