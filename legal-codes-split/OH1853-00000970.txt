The defendant may demur to the petition only when
it appears on its face, either,

1. That the court has no jurisdiction of the person of the
defendant, or the subject of the action:

2. That the plaintifff has not legal capacity to sue:

3. That there is another action pending between the
same parties for the same cause :

4. That there is a defect of parties, plaintifff or defendant:

5. That several causes of action are improperly joined :

6. That the petition does not state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action.
