When the jury has been sworn, the trial shall proceed in the following order, unless the court for special
reasons otherwise direct:

1. The plaintifff must briefly state his claim, and may
briefly state the evidence by which he expects to sustain it.

2. The defendant must then briefly state his defense,
and may briefly state the evidence he expects to offer in support of it. I

3. The party, who would be defeated if no evidence were
given on either side, must first produce his evidence ; the adverse party will then reduce his evidence.

4.. The parties wi then be confined to rebutting evidence,
unless the court, for good reasons in furtherance of justice,
permits them to offer evidence in their original case.

5. When the evidence is concluded, either party may request instructions to the jury on points of law, which shall be
given or refused by the court: which instructions shall be
reduced to writing, if either party require it.

6. The arties may then submit or argue the case to the
jury. In e argument, the party required first to produce
his evidence, shall have the opening and conclusion. If several defendants having se arate defenses, appear by different counsel, the court sha arrange their relative order.

7. The court may again charge the jury after the argument is concluded.
