The appeal shallbe made by serving on the adverse party or his attorney, and filing with the
clerk of the court in which the judgment is entered, a notice stating the appeal. The Judge who tried or
heard the cause, or one of the Justices of the Supreme Court, may order a stay of the proceedings upon
the judgment until the expiration of the time allowed for preparing and tiling a statement of facts. At
the time of tiling the notice, the appellant shall pay to the clerk his fees for making up the transcript of
the record in the case. Until his fees are paid, the clerk shall not be bound to receive or file the notice.
